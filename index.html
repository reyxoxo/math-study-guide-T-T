Unit One: Transformations

Translations: when you move a shape without rotating or resizing it. You can move it up, down, left, or right, or all four!

Refections: Flipping a shape/coordinate over the x or y axis.

If a point is refelcted over the x axis, the the y coordinates change signs. If a point is reflected over the y axis, the x values change signs. For example, (6,7) reflected over the x axis becomes (6,-7) and (6,7) reflected over the y axis becomes (-6,7).

Rotations refer to when a number is quite literally rotated around the origin (0,0)

Typically, counterclockwise rotations are used. The order goes:

90 degrees CCW: (-y,x)

180 degrees CCW: (-x,-y)

90 degrees CCW: (y,-x)

However, when its rotaed clockwise, its the opposite. Shocker!!

90 degrees CCW: (y,-x)

180 degrees CW: (-x,-y)

90 degrees CCW: (-y,x)

Dilations are next! Sigh...

Dilations mean to change the size of a shape, or change a point's placement on a graph. If it's a fraction (like 6/7) then the size of the shape shrinks. If it's a larger number, like 6 or 7, it grows!

For example, a point located at (6,7) dilated by 2 would be (12, 14). Likewise, the point (12,14) dilated by 1/2 would be (6,7).

Moving onto parent functions!!

The ones on the test are quadratic, sqare rooted, linear, exponential, absolute value, and cubed.

Fuctions always need to pass the vertical line test. Put a finger against the graph, and as you move it from left to right, no part of the fuction should come twice. Or, just make sure none of the x values repeat.

Traslations can be given to these fuctions by putting a number in front of them. For example, (x+6)^2 would mean to move the function of x^2 left 6 units.

The key thing about horizontal translations on parent functions as that they move the opposite way. From the origin, if you had the function (x+7)^2, it would move 7 units left. However, if you had (x-6)^2, it would move 6 units right.

Similar to how the translations move opposite ways, when a fuction is stretched or compressed, this also works. A fraction (6/7) stretches the fuction, while a number like 6 or 7 compresses the function.

Also, if a negative sign is placed before the fuction, it flips.
